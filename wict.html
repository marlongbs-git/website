Women in Clean Tech ASHRAE Building Design Competition

The ASHRAE Setty Family Foundation Net Zero Energy Design Competition challenged teams to design an energy-efficient public library in São Paulo, Brazil, minimizing life-cycle costs, environmental impact, and carbon footprint while maximizing occupant comfort and architectural integration.

As the team's electrical designer, I was responsible for the building's lighting system, including load calculations, fixture modeling, control automation, and full compliance with ASHRAE standards.

Key Achievements

  • Developed automated Excel tools to calculate and organize lighting power density across all building zones
  • Selected and specified energy-efficient fixtures using decision matrices evaluating cost, efficiency, and life cycle costs
  • Designed a lighting control system compliant with ASHRAE 189.1, incorporating occupancy sensors, daylight dimming, and scheduled shutoff

This project involved designing and analyzing the lighting system for a multi-story public library in São Paulo, Brazil, in compliance with ASHRAE Standard 90.1-2022. Using the Space-by-Space Method, each room was categorized to determine maximum allowable Lighting Power Density and control requirements, producing a code-maximum load of 10,720 W for the second floor and 54,158 W across the full building. Excel-based tools were developed to automate LPD calculations for each room based on dimensions and ceiling height. Fixtures were selected through decision matrices weighing energy efficiency, cost, product lifecycle, and installation flexibility.

LED surface-mounted fixtures were chosen for their efficiency and longevity, and the second floor was fully modeled in DIALux Evo using manufacturer-accurate fixture data and furniture layouts. Control strategies were designed to ASHRAE standards, incorporating daylight-responsive dimming, occupancy sensing, and scheduled shutoff. The modeled load for Level 2 came in at 3,165 W, well below the 10,720 W code maximum. Scaling this across the full building estimated a total load of roughly 10,845 W, about 80% below the theoretical maximum. Further savings are expected through daylight-responsive controls in curtain wall areas, and outdoor fixtures will be specified to minimize Backlight, Uplight, and Glare ratings.
